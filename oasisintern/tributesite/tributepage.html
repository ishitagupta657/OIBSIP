Albert Einstein

  • One of the greatest 20th-century scientists, mathematicians, and physicists was Albert Einstein.

  • At the age of 12, Einstein began studying calculus independently, and by the time he was 14, he was an expert in both integral and differential calculus.

  • At the age of 23, Albert graduated from college and was hired as a patent officer at the Federal Office for Intellectual Property in Bern, Switzerland.

  • While working there, he completed his Ph.D., after which he became a professor at the University of Zurich.

  • During this period he gave the theory of mass-energy (E = mc2).

  • He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1981 for his photoelectric effect.

  • His works are still studied in standard institutions of learning throughout the world.
